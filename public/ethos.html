ARC

Ethos

A personal velocity system. Three domains. One question. Every day.

Why Arc

An arc is a trajectory. Not a line — that's too rigid. Not a circle — that's going nowhere. An arc is movement with curvature. Direction that reveals itself over time.

You don't see the full arc until you've lived enough days. But each day, you feel whether you're bending toward something or drifting away from it.

Arc doesn't track habits. It doesn't count streaks. It asks one question and gives you an honest answer based on your own behavior. That's it.

"The app should feel like a sharp tool, not a friendly companion."
The Three Domains

Your life runs on three channels. Every day, you do one thing in each. Not everything. One thing.

Body

Physical. Movement. Health. Did you show up for your body today? A gym session, a run, a long walk. Not a stretch while watching TV — something deliberate.

Build

Output. Creation. Progress on the thing that matters. Shipped code, wrote the proposal, had the hard conversation at work. The thing that moves your craft forward by one unit.

Exposure

Social. Connection. Putting yourself in rooms. Dinner with a friend, networking call, community event. The channel most high-performers neglect — and the one that compounds the hardest.

How to Use Arc

Four touchpoints. That's your entire relationship with this app.

01
Morning Commit
Under 60 seconds. Every day.

Open Arc. Type your intent — one sentence that frames the day. Fill in one task per domain. Close the app.

Your intent is your contract. The three tasks are your evidence.
02
During the Day
Under 10 seconds. As tasks complete.

Open Arc. Swipe the completed card. Gold fill. Card locks. Close the app. The app never comes to you — you go to it.

Made a mistake? Long-press a completed card to undo.
03
Midnight
Automatic. You do nothing.

Day freezes. Whatever's done is done. Whatever's not is not. Cards become read-only. Tomorrow starts blank. No retroactive edits.

Missed a day entirely? That's a ghost day. The chart shows the gap.
04
Sunday Reflection
Once a week. The Velocity tab.

Tap Velocity. See your Monday through Sunday charted. See which domains are strong, which are lagging. See your status label. Adjust next week.

LOCKED IN / MOVING / DRAG DETECTED / FLATLINE — the label tells you the truth.
Velocity States

Your weekly average determines your state. No spin. No encouragement. Just signal.

Locked In 2.5 – 3.0 / day Executing across all three. Keep going.
Moving 1.5 – 2.4 / day In motion. One domain is lagging. Find it.
Drag Detected 0.5 – 1.4 / day Friction is winning. Something needs to change.
Flatline 0.0 – 0.4 / day Full stop. Start with one card tomorrow.
The Rules
Missed days are data.

Not failure. The chart shows gaps because gaps are real. Don't fill them in. Learn from them.

No retroactive edits.

If you didn't open the app, it's a ghost day. If you opened it but didn't finish, that's present but incomplete. Both are honest.

The app doesn't come to you.

No notifications. No reminders. No nudges. You open it because you choose to. That's the point.

Completing is easy. Uncommitting is hard.

Swipe to mark done. Long-press to undo. The friction is intentional — commitments should cost something to break.

One task per domain.

Not three. Not a sub-list. One measurable thing. If you can't name it in one line, you haven't decided what matters today.

Features are earned.

The app grows as you do. Prove the simple version works first.

"Am I moving, or am I drifting?"
